It’s 2024, Let's Talk About Overwhelm

II was to release this article before 1st Jan but due to ‘unavoidable circumstances’ called procrastination, here we are.

I am not great at big events because they always seem to overwhelm me. What do I mean? Well, I am not saying I don't like events, only those that are about me. Events that are centred around me always seem like a lot to take in, I end up getting overwhelmed, everything usually seems surreal, I end up losing track of time and before you know it, the day's over and we're prospecting again. If you have some idea of what I am talking about, welcome to the family, you're not alone. If you have no clue about any of this, first you're lucky and second, I hope this article gives you a glimpse of what it's like because it is sort of ‘ineffable’.

Being overwhelmed on such occasions has always been like a part of me, I've had to and still deal with it every time I am recognised for something. I may have been working hard in a specific niche or towards getting something and when all my efforts finally lead me to success, the success itself overwhelms me, I have this sort of emotional numbness and everything seems so surreal. The congratulations, the remarks, the cheer and the attention just seem like a lot to process. There is a sense of anxiety, impostor syndrome and surrealism in the air. It’s only after months fly by when you look back and view it as something big. I mean you did that!

I mean 2 months ago you made your first million or got your Masters, everyone was there but you didn’t feel the celebrations.

I have had about three or four ‘major event’ days in my life, those days I got celebrated or honoured for having achieved something. Most of them were academic, I have never exhaled at any sporting or community activity for that matter. I was also raised to know selflessness which moulded me to find comfort in shining a light on others, so I always liked shining the attention to others and moments where stuff was about me were rare, maybe just being top of the class in my primary school.

For the few ‘events’ that have been about me, I would always spend weeks if not months anticipating them and when they finally arrived, the fact that everyone was all about me was too much to take in because I was never used this much attention and the day would just fly by. I mean I'm so excited that I have achieved this but on top of all these other reasons, the fact that everyone else is happy and celebrating me makes me feel like a fraud somehow and that it's too much for this achievement. (A little bit of impostor syndrome).

I hope this awful description paints a picture of what I am trying to elaborate.

So here is a question for you, how often do these days pop up? You know, those days when everything is about you? I mean you live for these kinds of days and they don't come very often so why do you choose to let something you create in your head deter you from having fun on the one of the few days you deserve to?

So what should you do?

Well, here's what's been working for me so far. First, the fact that you recognise that you go through this is a ‘good enough’ first step. I tended to forget all the ‘bad times’ I had gone through when I finally achieved what I had been working towards. All the late nights I spent up, all the Saturdays I stayed home, and all the relentlessness would just fade.

So one of the ideas I tried implementing that seems to work for me is journaling. It's not a complex thing to do, I just keep a record of everything I do, every puzzle I stumble through, every shitty day, every mild- depression the daily tasks give me, all the litres of coffee I drink and every discomfort I get from my effort, all in a google sheet somewhere. So, fast forward when I have my Masters, or my first Million and I feel even a pinch of overwhelm or imposter syndrome creeping in, I just read through my journal, how rough it's been and how I deserve every attention and celebration for this moment. There are a lot of other ways like meditation etc but I found this one to be the most efficient for me.

You live for these moments, for these great achievements days so, always start them by finding calm amid all the chaos present both in your mind and externally. So as you set sail for your 2024 and that first reward/achievement comes through, pause and recall all the hard work, sweat, anxiety, a little depression and doubt that all compounded into that one glorious achievement. Actually, write some of them in your acceptance speech. Take a breath, look at it, hug it if you can, it's yours, you've earned it. If it's a recognition event, enjoy the chaos and noise that come with it.

Be in the moment.

You’re gonna have an epic 2024.

Peace
