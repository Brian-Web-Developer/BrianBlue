You need these skills to navigate 'The Awkward Dance'

You probably once walked down a street or any path and unintentionally collided or bumped into another person and then your efforts of struggling to navigate around each other resulted in repetitive movements in the same direction. I call this movement ‘Sync-ing’ because you are not Shore of the other person’s next move (If you did not get that joke, we can't be friends.) This is colloquially called ‘the Awkward Dance’. You are then accompanied by moments of confusion, hesitation, and mutual attempts to step aside simultaneously.

You are probably thinking that there's an easy straight-forward solution; Just pick one direction and stick to it and allow the other person to pass or probably stand still and still allow the other person to pass through. Well at the moment the easy choice is never easy. A stalemate arises when you’re both anticipating the same move or have the same idea, say when you step to the right, the other person follows in an attempt to clear the way for you.

So you’ve now both cleared the way but are still in a blockade and so you decide to jump into the cleared way but guess what, the other person also has that same idea and before you know it, you are stuck in this foot dance and the awkwardness is raising your anxiety and you feel like you are becoming a nuisance to the other person. At some point you’ll decide to look up to have a glimpse of the person you are dancing with and if it's a face that is not ‘socially appealing’ it can leave you with a mini-PTSD (Totally Pun-Intended). So there are a lot of theories that try to explain this mundane behaviour but there’s one I found most relatable to, Proxemics. In simple terms, proxemics is the study of personal space and what space people feel is comfortable for them. You can read more about Proxemics Here

So what do you do in this precarious state? Let me take you off track for a second. I am about to share a dark secret. I was in Standard 3 at Nyang’ori Primary School, tiny, innocent, in the staffroom picking up books from our maths teacher when it happened. Relax, I was not molested (I know that is what you were thinking). On my way out, the standard five class teacher at the time (I won't mention her name) put me on a hiatus on her way from a lesson. She filled the entire staffroom door in her attempt to get in and I could feel the air running as I stood there waiting for her to get through.

I knew I’d definitely die of asphyxiation if these conditions were to remain constant for a considerable time. I had to wait for her to squeeze both her hips through the door to leave the staffroom. Luckily (or Magically) she managed to enter without creating a crack in the wall (pun intended) but now I had to get around her to exit the premise. In an attempt to do so, she stepped in my direction and before I knew it, we were stuck doing the awkward dance. This was scary, I mean I could feel little tremors after she made a step. I was really scared of huge people, especially in confined spaces. I was a weird kid but in my defense, the big fish often eat the other fish.

After several failed attempts to create a roundabout around her, I dropped my gun like a real hero. I stood still and looked at her in an attempt to beg for mercy. In the spirit of sticking to ‘nicer words’, her face was not ‘Socially Appealing’. To break out of this irksome state of affairs, she grabbed my head with one hand (my whole head fit in her hand) and spun me around to her right hip and used the created space to pass through as she talked to another teacher in Maragoli. I ran back to class and 30 minutes later I could still feel her fingers on my head. I still cringe thinking about it now.

So ever since then, I decided to never get caught in the web of the awkward dance again and my job today is to equip you with the necessary tools and skills to utilise when such a situation arises. I have three tips for you, I call them thrips, get it? It’s a Portmanteau from the two… Never Mind. So the first tip, in this world there’s the big fish and just fish. The Big fish eat the other fish for survival. Be the big fish, stand your ground and the other fish will have to swim around you. So when caught in this spider web, don't shoot more webs, stand still and allow the other person to shoot theirs (This is personal Growth). By doing this, you guarantee the other person's web will work and you’re therefore the bigger fish (Enough with the Euphemisms and Metaphors). Tip two, (this works best if your face is not socially appealing or you occupy significant space), don't move! Let the other person wiggle around your hip. The last tip (this applies if their face is socially appealing), get their number because this is all the sparks you need you single and hopeless dumbo.

You have an Awesome Weekend

Peace
